Sashimi Rolls

Description

A sashimi roll usually refers to a sushi roll that features sashimi-grade fish (raw fish like salmon, tuna, or yellowtail) but with a twist: the fish might be layered on top of the roll instead of inside, or sometimes the roll itself might be made mostly of fish without a lot of rice or seaweed.

It's different from regular sashimi (which is just slices of raw fish) because a sashimi roll usually includes other ingredients like avocado, cucumber, or crab, rolled together with a little bit of rice and nori (seaweed) or sometimes even without seaweed, depending on the style.

It's basically the elegance of sashimi, combined with the creativity and structure of a sushi roll!

Ingredients

  • Sashimi-grade fish (like tuna, salmon, yellowtail, or snapper)
  • Sushi rice (lightly vinegared rice)
  • Nori (seaweed sheet) - sometimes optional
  • Avocado (optional)
  • Cucumber (optional)
  • Crab stick or real crab meat (optional)
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • Green onions or chives (optional)
  • Masago (fish roe) or tobiko (optional)
  • Spicy mayo or ponzu sauce (optional, for topping or dipping)

Steps

  1. Prepare the sushi rice
    • Cook short-grain rice, season it with rice vinegar, sugar, and a pinch of salt. Let it cool to room temperature.
  2. Slice the fish
    • Cut your sashimi-grade fish (like tuna or salmon) into thin slices for topping, and some smaller strips if you want fish inside the roll too.
  3. Prepare fillings
    • Slice avocado, cucumber, or any other fillings you want into thin strips.
  4. Set up the rolling station
    • Place a bamboo mat on a clean surface, cover it with plastic wrap to prevent sticking.
  5. Lay down the nori (optional)
    • If using seaweed, put a sheet of nori shiny side down on the mat.
    • If no nori, just sprinkle a thin layer of rice directly on the mat for a "no-seaweed" roll.
  6. Spread the sushi rice
    • Wet your fingers and gently spread an even, thin layer of rice over the nori or mat.
  7. Add fillings
    • Place strips of fish (optional), avocado, cucumber, or crab near the bottom edge of the rice.
  8. Roll it up
    • Use the mat to tightly roll everything into a log shape, pressing gently but firmly.
  9. Add sashimi slices on top
    • Lay your thin sashimi slices across the top of the roll.
    • Cover with plastic wrap and gently press again with the bamboo mat to shape it nicely.
  10. Slice and serve
    • Use a sharp, wet knife to slice the roll into bite-sized pieces.
    • Top with sauces (like spicy mayo or ponzu), masago, or sesame seeds if you want.
