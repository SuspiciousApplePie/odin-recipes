Sashimi Rolls

Description

A sashimi roll usually refers to a sushi roll that features sashimi-grade fish (raw fish like salmon, tuna, or yellowtail) but with a twist: the fish might be layered on top of the roll instead of inside, or sometimes the roll itself might be made mostly of fish without a lot of rice or seaweed.

It's different from regular sashimi (which is just slices of raw fish) because a sashimi roll usually includes other ingredients like avocado, cucumber, or crab, rolled together with a little bit of rice and nori (seaweed) or sometimes even without seaweed, depending on the style.

It's basically the elegance of sashimi, combined with the creativity and structure of a sushi roll!

Ingredients

  • Sashimi-grade fish (like tuna, salmon, yellowtail, or snapper)
  • Sushi rice (lightly vinegared rice)
  • Nori (seaweed sheet) - sometimes optional
  • Avocado (optional)
  • Cucumber (optional)
  • Crab stick or real crab meat (optional)
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • Green onions or chives (optional)
  • Masago (fish roe) or tobiko (optional)
  • Spicy mayo or ponzu sauce (optional, for topping or dipping)
