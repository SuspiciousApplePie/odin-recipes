Pizza

Description

Italian pizza is known for its simplicity, freshness, and balance. It features a thin, chewy crust made from just flour, water, salt, and yeast, often cooked at very high temperatures in a wood-fired oven.

The toppings are light and carefully chosen: fresh tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella cheese, a few basil leaves, and a drizzle of olive oil. Unlike heavier American styles, Italian pizza highlights the natural flavors of its ingredients, resulting in a light, aromatic, and slightly smoky dish. Each bite captures the essence of Italian cooking — pure, honest, and deeply satisfying.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (flour, water, salt, yeast, olive oil)
  • Tomato sauce (crushed fresh tomatoes, sometimes with a pinch of salt or oregano)
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese (like mozzarella di bufala or fior di latte)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Optional: a sprinkle of sea salt or a pinch of oregano

Steps

  1. Prepare the dough
    • Mix flour, water, salt, yeast, and a little olive oil into a dough.
    • Knead it until smooth and elastic.
    • Let it rise for 1-2 hours until it doubles in size.
  2. Preheat the oven
    • Heat your oven as hot as possible (ideally around 250°C / 480°F or more).
    • If you have a pizza stone, preheat it too.
  3. Shape the dough
    • Gently stretch or roll out the dough into a thin, round shape.
    • Try not to press out all the air to keep the crust light and bubbly.
  4. Add the toppings
    • Spread a thin layer of fresh tomato sauce over the dough.
    • Add slices of fresh mozzarella cheese.
    • Place a few basil leaves on top.
    • Drizzle with a little extra-virgin olive oil.
  5. Bake the pizza
    • Slide the pizza onto the hot stone or baking tray.
    • Bake for 7-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and slightly charred, and the cheese is melted and bubbling.
  6. Finish and serve
    • Add a final drizzle of olive oil if you like.
    • Slice and enjoy immediately!
