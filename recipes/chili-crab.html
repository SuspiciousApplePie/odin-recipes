Chili Crab

Description

Chili crab is a bold, flavorful dish that's a staple of Singaporean and Malaysian cuisine. This seafood delicacy features fresh crab (usually mud crab) cooked in a rich, savory, and slightly spicy tomato-based sauce. The sauce itself is a blend of chili, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, and tamarind, creating a tangy, sweet, and mildly spicy profile. The crab is typically stir-fried in the sauce, allowing the meat to soak up the delicious flavors while retaining its sweetness.

Often, the dish is served with mantou (fried buns) to dip into the flavorful sauce, making it a satisfying, hands-on meal. The richness of the sauce, combined with the sweet crab meat, creates a perfect balance of heat and sweetness — a true indulgence for those who love bold flavors.

Ingredients

For the crab

  • 1 large mud crab (or any fresh, meaty crab), cleaned and cut into pieces
  • A little cornstarch (optional, for light coating)

For the chili sauce

  • 2–3 tablespoons oil (for stir-frying)
  • 3–5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1–2 red chilies, chopped (adjust to your spice level)
  • 1 small piece ginger, minced
  • 2–3 tablespoons tomato ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce (like sambal oelek or bottled chili sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock or water
  • 1 teaspoon tamarind paste (optional, for tanginess)
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with a little water (for thickening)
  • 1 beaten egg (for swirling into the sauce at the end)

Optional (for serving)

  • Fried mantou buns or steamed rice
  • Spring onions or cilantro for garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare the crab
    • Clean and cut the crab into manageable pieces (claws, body halves, legs).
    • Lightly coat the crab pieces in a little cornstarch if you want them slightly crispier after frying (optional).
  2. Make the sauce base
    • Blend or finely chop garlic, chilies, and ginger into a rough paste.
    • Set aside the ketchup, chili sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, tamarind paste, and chicken stock you'll use these soon.
  3. Fry the crab
    • Heat oil in a wok or large pan over medium-high heat.
    • Stir-fry the crab pieces for a few minutes until they start turning red.
    • Remove the crab and set aside temporarily.
  4. Cook the sauce
    • In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed.
    • Stir-fry the garlic-chili-ginger paste until fragrant (about 1 minute).
    • Add ketchup, chili sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, tamarind paste, and chicken stock.
    • Stir well and bring to a simmer.
  5. Combine crab and sauce
    • Return the crab to the pan.
    • Toss to coat everything in the sauce.
    • Cover and simmer for 5–10 minutes, until the crab meat is fully cooked and the sauce thickens slightly.
  6. Thicken and finish
    • Stir the cornstarch slurry (cornstarch + water) into the sauce to thicken it.
    • Then slowly pour in the beaten egg while stirring gently to create silky ribbons in the sauce.
  7. Serve
    • Garnish with spring onions or cilantro.
    • Serve hot with fried mantou buns or steamed rice to soak up the delicious sauce!
